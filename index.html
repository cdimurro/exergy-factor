What it is

Exergy Factor is a quality number attached to an energy number.

Conventional reporting says how much energy exists or moves. Exergy Factor adds how much accessible work potential that energy carries at the declared boundary.

The reporting format is intentionally simple: quantity, f_X = value. For example, 1 MWh, f_X = 0.73 means one megawatt-hour of energy with 0.73 megawatt-hours of accessible work potential.

Methodology

For heat, this calculator uses the Carnot factor.

When the source and sink temperatures are provided, the calculator estimates thermal Exergy Factor as: 

f_X = 1 - T_sink / T_source

Temperatures are converted to kelvin before calculation. If you already know the Exergy Factor for electricity, fuel, solar radiation, storage, or another carrier, enter it directly and uncheck automatic temperature calculation.
